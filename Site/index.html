About Me

Who am I?

My name is Javier Taylor and I am a 19 year old aspiring software engineer studying Computer Science in my soon to be third year at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, MI. I grew up in Ypsilanti, MI, and my roots lie in the hills of Tennesse and in the Dominican Republic.

How did I get here?

Before coming to college I had never written a line of code in my life. I chose the University of Michigan because it was close, I loved the sports teams, and I thought I was going to be a mechanical engineer. But, when I took my first programming course, I realized that I had found something that I really enjoyed, so I threw myself in to it. I started going deeper in to the major, working in class and on my own to hone my programming abilities. I have worked to develop teamwork and leadership through group projects and organizations that I am a part of, and it has shaped the way that I look at development. I have taken courses ranging from entrepreneurial creativity to data structures and algorithms, and the more that I learn, the better I am at working through any problem that is thrown my way. I am currently a Software Engineering intern at Servant Systems Inc, a small company near Ann Arbor, MI.

What do I love to do?

I expect to graduate in April/May of 2018. I am interested in software engineering and entrepreneurship and their application to real world problems that lead to innovative solutions. I am keen on collaborative software development, and through coursework and extracurricular experience have gained the knowledge to design and implement clean, efficient, and robust code. I have extensive experience with leadership and teamwork, and recognize its importance to the software development process. Before college I had never written a single line of code, but I have since delved in to a passion for programming, and have pursued excellence in it ever since.
